What Should I Buy, and How Do I Justify It?

General Aquisitions Tips

There are many tools you can use for aquisitions Here are just a few:

  • Library Journal: offers reviews of major upcoming releases, with "starred reviews" for recommended titles.
  • Best Seller lists: identify current best sellers that your library missed on release.
  • NPR: often spotlights upcoming nonfiction titles.
  • Patron Requests: Can help to identify titles of particular patron interest
  • Library Programming: Obtain materials that tie into any adult programs scheduled.
  • Library Staff: Staff member have interests which may help in acquisitions decisions. Feel free to delegate targeted acquisitions as appropriate.

If your library does not already have a mechanism for patron purchase requests, then implement it. As this is generally a procedure and not a policy, you should not need Libary Board (or other decision-maker) approval in most cases.

Finally, be on alert for upcoming titles that are set in or around your community or geographic area. Even a title that would be considered "low interest" in most communities is likely to receive interest if it connects to your library's community.

Never forget that your first goal is to serve your patrons. Listening to them will be your most effective tool!
